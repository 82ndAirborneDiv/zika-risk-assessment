Zika Risk Assessment

Zika virus is primarily spread by mosquitoes. A pregnant woman can pass Zika to her developing fetus. Zika can also be spread through sex (vaginal, anal, or oral) by a man with Zika to his female and male partners (even if he doesn't have symptoms). Where you live, your travel history, and the travel history of your sex partner(s) can affect your risk of Zika. This web app can help you learn more about Zika and how to protect yourself.

  • Use this app for each traveler or resident in your household.
  • If you traveled recently and also have future travel plans, please answer questions for both scenarios to get complete CDC recommendations.
  • This app is not intended to replace the advice of a medical professional.

Zika Risk Assessment

  • The mosquitoes that spread Zika usually do not live at elevations above 6,500 feet (2,000 meters).
  • Travelers and residents whose activities are only above this elevation are at minimal risk of getting Zika from a mosquito.
  • For more information on Zika and elevation, see: http://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/q-a-zika-risk-high-elevations.
  • Check our travel notices for a map of your specific destination: http://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/zika-travel-information

CDC is routinely updating information as we learn more about Zika. Check the CDC website often for the latest information. http://www.cdc.gov/zika/index.html
