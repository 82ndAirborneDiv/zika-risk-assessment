Zika Risk Assessment Tool

Zika virus is primarily spread by mosquitoes. It can also be spread by a man with Zika to his sex partners, even if he doesn't get sick. Where you live, your travel history, and the travel histroy of your sex partner(s) can affect your risk of Zika. This web app can help you learn more about Zika and how to protect yourself.

  • Use this app for each traveler or resident in your household.
  • If you traveled recently and also have future travel plans, please answer questions for both scenarios to get complete CDC recommendations.
  • This app is not intended to replace the advice of a medical professional.

Zika Risk Assessment Tool

The mosquitoes that spread Zika usually do not live at elevations above 6,500 feet (2,000 meters). Travelers and residents whose activities are only above this elevation are at minimal risk of getting Zika from a mosquito. For more information: http://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/page/q-a-zika-risk-high-elevations

CDC routinely updates information as it learns more about Zika: Check CDC’s website often for the latest information. http://www.cdc.gov/zika/index.html CDC’s guidance for women and their partners thinking about pregnancy will continue to be updated as we learn more.
